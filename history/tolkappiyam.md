excluding vEda and purANa¹ the earliest known mention of the area is in the grammar book *tolkAppiyaM* by tolkAppiyar.

as per n. rAmEsaN, sAligrAma kRSNa rAmacandra rAo, and others, this is dated to the 1st century CE (they note it as after christ in their books). however, an extremely conservative estimate by historians state 2nd or 3rd century AD - but i digress.

admittedly, the reference in question is not in the tolkAppiyaM proper, but in panampAranAr’s foreword. however, the dating remains the same due to the fact that panampAranAr and tolkAppiyar were contemporaries. 

“வட வேங்கடம் தென் குமரி ஆயிடைத் தமிழ் கூறும் நல் உலகத்து”

vaTa venGkaTam tEn kumari AyiTait tamiZh kUrum nal ulakattu

tamiZh-speaking land was extended between vemGaDam (modern tirupatI) on the north and  kumari (kanya kumari) on the south.

so tirumala (the hill/location) definitely existed even in archaic times, although the AlayAm isn't (explicitly) mentioned

tl:dr, tirumala is first mentioned as vemGaDaM in a 1st century tamiZh grammar book , as a border for tamil country

¹i say this from a purely historical point-of-view. my intention was not to do sAstra-ninda
